## Welcome home

You were in hospital from **14 May to 18 May 2025**. This leaflet explains what happened, what treatment you had, and what to do next. Please read it carefully and keep it somewhere safe.

## What was wrong

You had a **stroke (acute ischaemic stroke)**. This means a blood clot blocked blood flow to part of your brain. It caused the drooping on the right side of your face, weakness in your right arm, and difficulty with speech that brought you in.

You also have a heart rhythm condition called **atrial fibrillation (AF)**. This was already known about before you came in. It is linked to the stroke.

---

## What was done in hospital

**Clot-busting treatment**
On the day you arrived, doctors gave you a medicine through a drip to dissolve the clot. This is called **thrombolysis (alteplase)**. You had a brain scan first to make sure it was safe to give.

**Feeding tube**
At first, your swallow was not safe, so a thin tube was passed through your nose into your stomach **(nasogastric feeding)** to make sure you got enough nutrition. By day three your swallow had recovered and the tube was removed.

**Physiotherapy and getting moving**
The physiotherapy team helped you start moving again. By day four you were up and about, though you still had some mild weakness in your right arm.

**Stroke rehabilitation team**
A team called the **Early Supported Discharge (ESD) team** came to see you. They have agreed to support you at home as you continue to recover.

---

Please read this section carefully. Some of your medicines have changed.
