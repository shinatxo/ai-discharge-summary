## What brought you into hospital

You came in on **10 May 2025** with stomach pain, a swollen tummy, vomiting, and no bowel movements. A scan confirmed that part of your small bowel (the tube that digests food) was blocked by scar tissue (adhesions) — most likely left over from an operation you had on your appendix in the past.

---

## What we did

At first, the team tried to treat the blockage without an operation. This involved resting your bowel, passing a thin tube through your nose into your stomach to drain it (a nasogastric tube), and giving you fluids through a drip.

When this did not work and it became clear that part of your bowel was no longer healthy, you needed an emergency operation (a laparotomy) on **12 May 2025**. During the operation, the surgeons:

- cut away the scar tissue that was causing the blockage (adhesiolysis),
- removed the unhealthy section of bowel (small bowel resection), and
- joined the healthy ends back together (anastomosis).

To protect that join while it heals, the surgeons also created a **loop ileostomy**. This is a small opening on your tummy wall where a section of bowel is brought to the surface. Your bowel waste now passes into a bag attached to this opening, rather than through your back passage. This is likely to be temporary — the possibility of reversing it will be discussed at your follow-up appointment.

After the operation you were cared for in the High Dependency Unit (HDU). By **15 May** you were eating, moving around, and your stoma was working well. By the time you went home on **18 May** you were managing your stoma independently.
