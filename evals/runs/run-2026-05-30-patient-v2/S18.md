# Your Discharge Letter

**From the Neurology Team | Admitted: 19 May 2025 | Discharged: 20 May 2025**

---

## What happened

You were admitted to hospital after having a seizure (a fit) at work. During the seizure your whole body shook. It lasted about 2 minutes. Afterwards you were briefly confused, which is normal after this type of seizure. A colleague saw it happen.

This type of seizure is called a **generalised tonic-clonic seizure**. It appears to have happened without a clear cause, so it is called an **unprovoked seizure**.

---

## Tests you had

While you were in hospital, the team ran several tests to look for a cause:

- **Blood tests** — normal
- **Blood sugar (glucose)** — normal
- **Drug and toxicology screen** — nothing found
- **CT scan of your head** — normal

Two more tests are still to come. These will be arranged for you as outpatient appointments (meaning you do not need to stay in hospital):

- **MRI scan of your brain**
- **EEG** (a test that measures the electrical activity in your brain)

---

## Treatment

The doctors decided not to start any anti-seizure medicines at this stage. This is the usual approach after a first seizure. The decision about whether you need medicine will be made after your outpatient tests and your clinic appointment.

---

## Medicines

You were not given any new medicines. You were not taking any medicines before you came in, and you have no known drug allergies.

---

## What happens next

You have three follow-up steps being arranged for you:

1. **First-fit clinic** — a specialist neurology outpatient appointment to review your seizure and plan your care.
2. **Outpatient MRI brain scan** — you will receive an appointment for this.
3. **Outpatient EEG** — you will receive an appointment for this.

Your **GP has been told** about your admission. They will wait for the results of your tests and the neurology clinic review before taking any further action.

---

## Important advice for now

Please follow this advice until you have been seen in the first-fit clinic:

- **Do not drive.** You must stop driving straight away. You must also **tell the DVLA** yourself. For a standard (Group 1) driving licence, you must not drive for **6 months from the date of your seizure (19 May 2025)**. This is subject to review by the neurology team. Your GP can also talk you through this if you have questions.
- **Do not swim alone.**
- **Do not work at heights.**
- **Do not have unsupervised baths.** It is safer to shower instead.

---

## When to get help

If you become unwell or have another seizure, contact your GP or call **NHS 111**.

Call **999** if it is an emergency.
